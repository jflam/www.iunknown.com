Entertainment PC's Part 2
2001-12-25

My foray into Entertainment PC's had a happy ending this Christmas afternoon. I successfully setup a brand new EPC at my parent's house, and everything more-or-less worked out of the box. Here's a low-down on the equipment list:

  1. A home-brew 1.6Ghz Pentium-4 running on an ECS P4ITA2 motherboard.
  2. 256MB RDRAM
  3. 60GB Western Digital HDD
  4. ATI All-In-Wonder RADEON card
  5. SoundBlaster Audigy Gamer card
  6. Viewsonic PF815 22" monitor
  7. Logitech Wireless Cordless Freedom Pro Wireless Keyboard and Mouse
  8. LinkSys WUSB11 Wireless 802.11b USB adapter

I was so impressed by this setup. The DVD playback was flawless on the Viewsonic monitor. Qualitatively, the TV display on the Viewsonic was as good as a Panasonic GAOO television that was tuned to the same station in the same room. Once we got the AC-3 outputs of both the ATI All-In-Wonder RADEON card and the Soundblaster Audigy cards connected to our Sony STR-DE575 receiver, we were able to hear Dolby Digital sound playback not only of DVD movies, but of some of the excellent AC3 samples that ship with the Soundblaster Audigy card. The SnapStream personal video recorder software worked on quite well, and the Near-DVD quality MPEG-4 encoding mode provided excellent quality recordings of live television.

My previous rant about EPC's reflected my highly negative experience with an EPC that used a TV as its output. I stand by my orignal position: the TV output is simply not good enough to be even low-level consumer quality. It doesn't even approach the quality that you would expect from home theatre equipment.

I look forward to the day when I can actually swap out my existing video card for one that is dedicated to providing excellent video output on a TV. After all, if the DVD manufacturers can do this, why can't the PC video card manufacturers do this? Come on, step up and take the challenge!
