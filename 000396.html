Living with Virtual PC 2004

I've been spending a lot of time repaving my laptop with Windows XP and switching to a Virtual PC lifestyle. So far, I've got two Windows XP machines setup, one with VS.NET 2003 and one with Whidbey. As I write this entry, I'm dropping Win2K3 EE onto another VM.

So far I'm very happy with the perf of VPC 2004. When running in full screen mode, I really can't notice the difference between VPC 2004 and native.

Peter Provost has an excellent FAQ on VPC 2004. Peter taught me the most excellent trick of installing MSDN onto your host OS and doing a network install of MSDN onto your guest OS. This saves a huge amount of space in your guest OS images.

I'm also writing this entry using BlogJet. It's a stop-gap tool until I get NewsGator 2.0 and MovablePoster configured again on my laptop.
