Life with Apple hardware

I actually had to get a bit of work done at the cottage last week. But I did something really dumb before I returned from my visit to HP the week before: I left behind the power adapter for my Thinkpad! Now, that's the quickest way that I can think of to turn your laptop into a useless pile of metal!

I reverted to my "backup" laptop - my G4 TiBook. Since I was mostly doing writing, I could get by with just Office and Freemind (which runs on Java). I took a bunch of photos as well, so I "developed" them using Photoshop. All in all, it was quite a pleasant experience. I burned the cursor movement keystrokes into my brain by the end of the week, so (at least with Word) I felt quite comfortable using the Mac.

When I passed through Denver, I dropped by the Apple store and bought a shiny new 30GB iPod. My first impressions: the packaging is beautiful and the headphones suck. They literally stopped working within about 2 hours! The amount of distortion was unbelievable. I bought a pair of $9 headphones from Wal-Mart that worked well until I got my Sony NC-11 noise-canceling headphones. I had a pair several years ago before I broke / lost it (long story) but they work extremely well. Thanks to Charlie from my class who ordered it for me (another long story).

I ripped some of my CD's using MP3 VBR 100% quality. Don't do this. I plan on re-ripping using a high constant bit-rate. The iPod has some strange bugs that cause VBR MP3's to stop short of the end of the song. Also, you won't know how long a song is because it can't calculate the length of a VBR song (strange since MusicMatch doesn't have a problem with this). And one other thing: playlists are everything on portable players with giant capacity. Spend the upfront time to organize your music and you will greatly increase your enjoyment.
