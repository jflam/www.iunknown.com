About me

My name is John Lam. I'm a software developer first and foremost. I have shipped eight commercial products that I know of. If you look at that list of products, you'll notice that there was a conspicuous absence of any activity from 1986 until 1995. That was because I was busy working on my other life.

I've spent most of my time since 1997 educating the software development community. I've co-authored two books, and authored or co-authored many articles for various publications. My sort-of up-to-date bibliography can be found here.

From 1997 to 2000, I worked at DevelopMentor. I started off there as a COM instructor, like most of the other folks who joined towards the end of the 1990's. I then left the comfortable surroundings of COM and ventured into the murky waters of the web and XML. I authored and maintained DevelopMentor's popular Essential Web Applications class from 1998 until the time I left. Along the way, I got roped into XML and contributed to DevelopMentor's Essential XML class, and co-authored Essential XML with Don Box and Aaron Skonnard.

Since April of 2001, I've been doing my training work with Wintellect. I co-authored our Programming ASP.NET class with Jeff Prosise. There are some really smart guys at Wintellect: Jeffrey Richter, John Robbins, Franceso Balena, and Dino Esposito are some of the names that you've probably heard of.

I left DevelopMentor at the end of 2000 because I wasn't happy with the fact that I hadn't shipped anything besides conference talks, slide decks, articles and books since 1997. I was itching to ship something that other people actually use in their work.

A chance meeting with Hugh Teegan at the Speaker's event at the WinSummit conference in October of 2000 confirmed the path that my life was going to take. Hugh works at Microsoft. He also co-developed Transactional Active Server Pages. Hearing the excitement in his voice as he described the process of shipping the product really reaffirmed my feelings that I really need to develop software again. That talk also made it much easier for me to stick to my guns when I had to have my chat with Don Box at the end of November when I announced my intention to leave DevelopMentor (Don's a very persuasive guy).

So, what's next? Here's my plan for the next couple of years.
