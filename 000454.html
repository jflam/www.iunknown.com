It shipped

My last two laptops were IBM Thinkpads: a 600E and a T23. Thinkpads are extremely well-built laptops and they hold up at least as long as their warranty. Once their warranty expires (3 years), things start breaking - displays, keyboards, and the TrackPoint among other things.

This time around, I couldn't go with a Thinkpad. They simply lag too far behind in features compared to Dells. So I'm trading IBM build quality for Dell features - I'm hoping I'm making the right decision here. But oh what features - the Dell web site just informed me that they finally shipped my new Inspiron 8600:

  • WUXGA, baby 1920×1200 15.4" wide-screen display
  • Pentium M 745 (that's the 1.8GHz, 90nm "Dothan" w/ 2MB L2 cache and 23W power dissipation)
  • 2GB DDR RAM
  • Hitachi 60GB 7200rpm disk
  • ATI Radeon 9600 Pro w/128MB VRAM

The thing that I'm really lusting after is that display - it's about 150ppi which should make it a fantastic Longhorn machine. The 2GB RAM should make it an excellent VPC machine. I'm buying an external 2.5" USB2 HDD enclosure so I have an extra spindle to run VPC on. The big 72Wh battery combined with the same power dissipation as the Pentium III-M on my T23 should result in about double the battery life.

It should arrive early next week. I can't wait ...
