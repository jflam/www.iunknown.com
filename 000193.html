Software co-operative

I spent some time this morning reading Po Bronson's web site. I really loved one of his earlier books, The Nudist on the Late Shift, which captures the spirit of Silicon Valley in the midst of the .com boom of the late 90's. What brought me back to his site is a new book that he just released, What Should I Do With My Life? I read an excerpt from this book in Fast Company, and it has me hooked. This will be the next non-geek book on my reading list.


While reading about his history, I read his fascinating description of a writer's cooperative called The Grotto in San Francisco. This idea really has me stoked. A few years ago, my friend Michael Lenaghan graciously offered me free office space in Toronto. I have fond memories of the years that I spent hanging out there, and one of our discussions there directly lead me to becoming an instructor at DevelopMentor and being exposed to the CLR for the first time.


These days, I work mostly out of my home office. However, it can be a very isolating experience. On days when I'm on a roll, it doesn't matter much where I am. However, on those other days, it can be a tremendous disadvantage.


So here's what I want to set up: a software co-operative based in Toronto. It would involve renting space either in a traditional office building or renting out an entire house. The members of the co-op would rent individual rooms in the office, and we would share common expenditures such as electricity, heating, and Internet access. Included in the cost would be some shared facilities like a kitchen and some sort of common room. It would be located in a transit-friendly place, most likely in the downtown core of Toronto.


The purpose of the co-operative would be to have a friendly place where people can go and get work done. It is not going to be anything more than that. The goal is NOT to create an umbrella "corporate" organization. It's really all about getting a bunch of people with shared interests together in a common space.


If you live in Toronto, and work as an independent generating some kind of computer code, please get in contact with me. By generating "some kind of computer code" I mean stuff like (but not restricted to):



  • C# / VB.NET managed development
  • Java
  • Flash
  • C++
  • VB
  • x86 assembler
  • Cocoa / Carbon on Mac OS
  • PHP
  • Python, Perl, Ruby
  • Lisp, Smalltalk

I would love to have a community of folks from different backgrounds. This would let us learn from each other and expand our collective horizons. It would be great to get a mixture of Computer Science researchers together with Flash developers. It would be great to have C# developers talking to Python developers. It would be great to have Mac OS developers talking to Windows developers. It's about celebrating what we have in common instead of letting marketing labels like "Linux", "Windows", and "Mac OS" divide us.
