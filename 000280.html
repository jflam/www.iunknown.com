ActiveSMART

I just downloaded a copy of ActiveSMART after my BIOS told me to replace my HDD on my desktop computer. The only reason I noticed this was because I had to replace the power supply in my desktop.1

If you're like a lot of other people I know, you tend to not perform hard resets of your computer very often (SMART diagnostics only run on hard resets, not software-initiated reboots). Unfortunately, if this is the case, you'll find that your BIOS S.M.A.R.T. diagnostics aren't actually all that useful since it could easily be months between hard resets of my computer.

I found that this utility does a great job of telling me exactly what my HDD's are up to on my computer. It also helps to demystify for me exactly what S.M.A.R.T. diagnostics actually are. I've also got it set up to email me alerts if one of my computer's HDD's are about to go south (it polls your HDD's diagnostics every hour). It's well worth the $24.95.

[1] I just installed an Enermax EG-365P-VE, which does a great job of cooling the case with its TWO built in fans.
