Baby Boot Camp

Inspired by being at Fort Sill for a week, we decided to begin "baby boot camp" when I returned. Matthew's sleep habits have been, um, not so good shall we say - and recently they took a turn for the worse. Carolyn's been worn down to the bone - especially having to deal with my being sick and then away for a week.



p. On Saturday night we decided to let him cry it out. After reading from cover to cover Healthy Sleep Habits, Happy Child we watched for drowsiness signs, settled him down by a bit of rocking, put him into his crib, kissed him goodnight and walked out of the room. As expected, there was some protesting. But it only lasted 9 minutes before there was ... silence. After checking in on him and making sure he was sleeping soundly we declared victory - prematurely.

An hour later he awoke and protested for an hour and twenty minutes! That was really difficult to take, but eventually he settled down for another 3 hours. He awoke for two feeds - at 12am and 4am, protested 5 minutes and 30 minutes respectively before awaking at 7:00am - his normal wake-up time. He was his normal happy self in the morning, much to our relief!

Last night, he was even better. He slept from 8pm until almost 3am before he woke up to feed just once. After, he protested for what seemed like an eternity - another 40 minutes, but he clearly got more sleep on the second night than the first night.

Matthew's definitely learning how to fall asleep by himself, and he's also learning how to fall asleep after awaking in the middle of the night - we'll hear brief yelps occasionally, but they're becoming less frequent. With any luck, we should have a more rested family soon!

Every child is different but here's my observations.

My daughter ( now 14 mos ) slept through the night at 8 months, and only woke once a night from 6-8 months old.

Two things which are paramount:
1. They MUST get enough calories during the day so that hunger doesn't wake them. This may have accounted for the long crying period.
2. The must not get too much or too little sleep during the day. Too much and they don't need a full nights rest. Too little and they become overtired ,powersleep a few hours and wake somewhat rested.

Hope this helps.
