Moving to Movable Type

Late last night, I finished doing an experimental [internal] port of my site over to Movable Type. I'd like to thank Brian Cantoni for his CityScript code frag that helped to bootstrap the process. Watch out for the fact that the status is set to Draft and not Publish; had I actually read Brian's entry I would have realized this :) Mea culpa.


I've been very happy using CityDesk for the past year and a half. However, I'm at the stage where I really want the TrackBack and PingBack features offered by a more mature (read: more than a big pile of static HTML and images) blogging infrastructure. A secondary goal is to regain control over my comments; I'd really like to see Google associate my comments with my URL.


There are two things that I still have to do:



  1. Move my Haloscan comments over to Movable Type. I don't really see an easy way of pulling this one off. Suggestions would be welcome.
  2. Remap existing URL's to Movable Type's archive URL's. This is the higher priority of the two items since I don't want to break other external links to my site. Since the site is currently running on a friend's Free BSD box, I suspect I'll just have to write the .htaccess file that remaps the URL's. This article seems to have just enough information to help bootstrap that process.

Hopefully I can get most of the move done by the end of this week.


I spend about 50% of my time working on my laptop (which implies disconnected access at some random coffee shop in the Greater Toronto Area), so really need to have an offline blog editor. I took a quick peek at w.bloggar last night, and it looks quite promising.
