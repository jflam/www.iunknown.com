While wandering around in the OS X alternate universe ...
2003-01-05

I discovered Glenn Vanderburg's blog. This is such a useful treasure-trove of insightful observations and links. I have found several entries that have made me stop and think. That's probably the highest compliment that I can pay anyone. Among the things that made me stop and think are:

  • Learning the history of engineering and understanding where software engineering sits on the path to maturity.
  • The role of specialists vs. generalists.
  • Some useful pointers to some recent work by Ward Cunningham on Software Archaeology.

Being exposed to new ideas and different ways of looking at old ideas is such a useful thing.

One useful perk for folks that work at traditional jobs would be to set aside a certain amount of time for personal research projects. I have some friends who work as biological chemists in the pharmaceutical industry and forward-looking companies like Merck, let them use 15% of their time on personal research projects. It should be noted that this kind of research, unlike CS research, cannot be done at home :)
