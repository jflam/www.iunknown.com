Photos from Mt. Rainier
2002-09-16

I was teaching my Programming ASP.NET class out in Seattle this past week. I had a great class, the students were unusually experienced and very enthusisatic.

The weather in Seattle this week was nothing short of spectacular. Brilliant blue skies the entire week. When Friday arrived, I was getting worried that my weather luck might run out, but the weather stayed awesome until Sunday, when I flew home.

On Friday afternoon, I drove to Mount Rainier National Park. There, I took a bunch of photographs, which I've uploaded to my PBase gallery. The first photograph, the sunset one, was taken on my drive to Mt. Rainier. I was going to miss sunset on the mountain because of nasty traffic delays, so I stopped off along the way and managed to get this great photograph of the sun setting over an inlet whose shores were covered with tree stumps.

Most of the photographs in the gallery were taken using filters. Just before my trip, I purchased some Cokin 1 and 2 stop "Grey" Graduated filters (models A120 and A121). These are as close to "Neutral Density" (color-neutral) filters that I could get without shelling out a whole pile of money, and they were able to expand the dynamic range of my photographs enough to show some detail in the photos that would otherwise be pure white or black.
