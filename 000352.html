What should blog entries be?

I've written some fairly long (1000+ word) pieces on my blog. I've seen other folks long pieces on their blogs as well (cbrumme's blog is the best example of this).Â I'm now convinced that blogs are horrible for long entries. Entries should be one or two paragraphs long with some links. That's it. Longer essay-style pieces belong in a different medium.

My vote is for PDF docs that are formatted for on-screen reading. This means acknowledging that folks hate scrolling and that 99.99% of the population have their monitor setup in landscape mode. Comments?

I totally disagree. One of the chief advantages of blogging is that the form places no constraints on length. As a writer you're free to write just enough to say what you want. If that's two paragraphs, fine. If what you have to say is interesting enough to your readers, then, 10,000 words is fine (Chris Brumme's posts have no wasted verbiage, and are fascinating to at least a few of us).

At its best, blogging occurs at the intersection of the public and private. It's the equivalent of the office water cooler chit-chat or discussions at the pub. While it lacks the fact-checking, editing, and legal vetting that go into "professional" publication, it has immediacy, personality, and honesty that are often missing from more structured forms.

I'm really dismayed at your suggestion of PDF as a format for on-screen reading. PDF is a vile format for on-screen reading. HTML is a pretty decent (and widely deployed) format for on-screen reading. The Microsoft Reader format(.LIT) is marginally better for book-length material, but there's not much tool support for the casual author. The new Microsoft Word Reading Layout view is pretty good as well, but Office 2003 doesn't have enough market penetration to warrant using that.
