One last hurrah

I'm writing this entry via NewsGator on my desktop PC back home after VPN'ing to my home network from the wireless access point at the Air Canada Lounge. I'm still amazed at how well all of this technology works.

I'm in for a special treat today. For all of the flying that I've done over the past 7 years, I've never flown on the second floor of a 747. Apparently Air Canada is going to retire its 747 fleet (6 planes) soon. So unless I'm flying on a Pacific flight with some other airline, this might be my last chance to fly on one of these planes.

One other thing. If there are any Air Canada Elite flyers reading this, you should notice that they've changed the requirements on the Aeroplan System Wide certificates - you can now use them on Y, M, H, and V fares. This is a lot different than last year when you could only use them on Y fares. So if you don't plan on flying to Europe, you can burn these upgrade certificates on North American flights now.
