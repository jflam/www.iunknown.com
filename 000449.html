Seriously scary stuff

Over lunch one day, an attendee who attended the Jesper Johansson's "How to get your Network hacked in 10 easy steps" talk scared the h*** out of me. Some really scary observations:

  • How powerful pre-computed hashtable attacks are. Apparently you can crack 10 character passwords stored in the SAM in 1 minute via pre-computed hashtable attacks. This is a very powerful motivation for introducing salted hashes into the SAM.
  • Pointers to where you can download rootkits. There's some interesting discussions there on how to hide processes from task manager / user mode code.
  • One particularly scary attack is the VideoCardKit. In this (theoretical?) attack, the subversive code hides in flash memory of various pieces of hardware on your computer: BIOS, video BIOS, HDD controller BIOS etc. Wow.
