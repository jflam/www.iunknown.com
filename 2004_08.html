Security thoughts

Robert Hensing works on the PSS Security team at Microsoft. He has just started blogging, and has some excellent thoughts on his blog based on his observations as a member of the Security Incident Response team at PSS. He has blogged about the effectiveness of pass phrases in lieu of passwords as well as identifying different hacker personas.

His advanced hacker persona category linked to this article on MSNBC that interviews a hacker who's a member of an organized crime team in Russia. It's interesting to note that the hacker was still doing remote attacks against Western corporations.

One scenario that has me particularly worried is planting a rogue administrator inside of one of those Western corporations. That rogue administrator could compromise systems by installing a particularly nasty variant of a rootkit- one that hides in the Flash BIOS of various hardware cards installed in servers. By the time the crime had been committed, the rogue admin would be long gone. It's a simple low-tech attack that one could easily imagine a member of a foreign intelligence service carrying out. It's a scary world out there.

Infrastructure Stories Part 2

After some helpful folks commented on my poor choice of a RAID0 solution on my desktop computer (which is now semi-decommissioned) I decided to rebuild my server with a more robust drive array. I went out and purchased a Promise SX4 RAID 5 SATA controller and 4 160GB Seagate SATA drives. As expected, 4 7200rpm drives are quite noisy, but thankfully the server is now located in my basement (more on this later).

I'm thinking about adding four Antec HDD coolers with built-in temperature gauges to my server because I have a store credit for an extra component that I no longer need. Now that would look just way too cool.

Infrastructure Stories Part 1

I've been a big fan of SpamBayes for some time now. Until recently, I've had a desktop computer running Outlook constantly so that SpamBayes can filter the incoming email off of my Exchange Server.

In the process of building some new infrastructure (more on this later) I've decided to consolidate servers. So I now have Outlook installed on my Exchange Server, and I use a disconnected RDP session to run Outlook and the Outlook SpamBayes plug-in. Works like a charm, and I have one less computer consuming power in my house.
