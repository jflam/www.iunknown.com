Social Computing

I believe that digital images are one of the key aspects of social computing. Recently, I saw Dare Obasanjo write about his social software epiphany. It looks like Google has a leg up on Microsoft in this area. Their recent acquisition of my favorite photo gallery software company, Picasa, is further evidence of this.

One of the really exciting things that will appear soon is peer-to-peer photo sharing. The Microsoft Research Wallop project is one possible approach. MSR recently published an article called The Life of a Digital Photo which collects together some really interesting scenarios, including Wallop.

I'm not convinced that we need a new operating system to enable a richer user experience with digital photos. The eye candy will help, but I really believe that most if not all of the technologies for making this happen already exist today. We just need some clever software that will make this happen (and I have no doubt that there's lots of companies operating in "stealth" mode today on just this problem).
